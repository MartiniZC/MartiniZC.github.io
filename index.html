Marine Energy Harvester · Pelton Turbine & Generator Assembly (Senior Design)

Goals

  • Design a buoy-mounted Pelton turbine and generator assembly that converts high-head water flow into electrical power.
  • Create a layout that uses realistic components (bearings, pulleys, fasteners) and can be manufactured with common processes.
  • Produce drawings and GD&T that communicate fit and alignment requirements clearly to a shop floor.

Process

  • Owned the CAD for the Pelton turbine wheel, final shaft assembly, generator housing, and pulley arrangement in SolidWorks.
  • Defined datum structures and applied functional GD&T to shaft journals, bearing seats, mounting faces, and housing interfaces.
  • Considered assembly order and maintainability when locating bearings, seals, and fasteners, using standard sizes wherever possible.
  • Coordinated with teammates performing hand calculations and checks so that the CAD geometry reflected design assumptions (head, flow, speed).

Results

  • Delivered a complete CAD assembly suitable for manufacturing review, including part drawings, assembly views, and BOM.
  • Documented alignment and fit requirements using GD&T so that critical interfaces (e.g., shaft to bearing, housing to frame) are inspectable.
  • Showcased a clear design story from concept to manufacturable model, highlighting my ability to own mechanical design and documentation.
FSAE Front & Rear Wing Concept · Aero Package Refinement

Goals

  • Evaluate aerodynamic performance of a multi-element wing in SolidWorks CFD and examine its on-track performance in OptimumLap.
  • Improve the baseline performance through redesign and validate the gains.
  • Develop an intuition for key aerodynamic behaviors so designs remain practical for FSAE implementation.

Process

  • Benchmarked the existing wing by setting up a CFD environment and running mesh-convergence studies to find a good fidelity/runtime balance.
  • Established a performance baseline by running the stock aerodynamics through OptimumLap to determine a lap-time target.
  • Selected a low-Reynolds-number airfoil (S1223) suitable for FSAE operating conditions and integrated it into the front-wing assembly.
  • Built an initial three-element geometry with conservative AoA, stagger, and spacing to allow systematic iterative refinement.
  • Conducted iterative CFD analysis, adjusting element AoA, gap, and overlap to balance downforce, drag, and stall behavior while managing ride-height sensitivity.
  • Evaluated flow quality using velocity, pressure, and shear-stress plots to identify separation, wake interaction, and slot-jet performance.
  • Simulated the final design in OptimumLap to quantify on-track benefit relative to the baseline vehicle.

Results

  • Achieved a significant downforce increase (CL improved from –2.6 to –3.18) while managing drag growth (CD from 1.09 to 1.39).
  • Identified and mitigated laminar separation bubbles and wake-induced losses through geometry and AoA refinement.
  • Generated a 1.00-second reduction in lap time in OptimumLap versus the baseline configuration.
Parametric V6 Engine CAD Assembly

Goals

  • Practice building and organizing a complex mechanical assembly entirely in CAD, with emphasis on design intent and parametric control.
  • Capture realistic relationships between major engine components (block, heads, pistons, rods, crankshaft, valvetrain).
  • Demonstrate speed and discipline in SolidWorks modeling and assembly management.

Process

  • Created individual parts for the engine block, heads, pistons, connecting rods, crankshaft, and valvetrain using fully defined sketches.
  • Used consistent reference planes and parametric dimensions so that bore, stroke, and bank angle can be adjusted without breaking the model.
  • Assembled components with a systematic mating strategy to maintain clear kinematic relationships between crank, rods, and pistons.
  • Organized the feature tree with meaningful names and folders, making the model easier to review and modify.

Results

  • Completed a functional V6 assembly that can be reconfigured without major rebuilds, illustrating strong design intent.
  • Showed capability to manage part–to–part relationships in a dense assembly and keep geometry robust as parameters change.
  • Provides a strong example of my CAD habits for complex mechanical systems, beyond simple brackets or single parts.
